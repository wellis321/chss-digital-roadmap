Digital Roadmap SWOT Analysis

Strategic Assessment for Head of Digital, Data & IT Role

Strategic Overview

A comprehensive analysis of CHSS's digital transformation roadmap

Strengths

Solid technical foundation with D365, established digital assets, and clear strategic alignment with organisational goals.

Weaknesses

System integration challenges, resource constraints, and complex governance requirements need attention.

Opportunities

AI integration potential, sustainability initiatives, and enhanced service delivery through digital innovation.

Threats

External pressures, technology risks, and implementation challenges that require careful management.

Overall Summary

CHSS has a strong foundation and clear vision for digital transformation. The roadmap is ambitious but achievable with proper execution. Success will depend on maintaining focus on user needs, managing organisational change effectively, and leveraging the organisation's strong reputation and partnerships in delivering world-class supported self-management.

Strengths

Core advantages and assets that position CHSS well for digital transformation

Solid Technical Foundation

  • Microsoft D365 Implementation: Successfully deployed case management system - scalable platform capable of supporting integrated self-management tool
  • Established Digital Assets: Functional websites, e-learning modules, mobile app framework (Talk with Me - ready for redeployment), and volunteer platform (Assemble)
  • Data Infrastructure: PowerBI reporting capabilities and ongoing data warehouse development

Strategic Alignment

  • Clear Vision: Digital roadmap directly supports "No Life Half Lived" strategy with quantified goals (175,000 additional people reached)
  • User-Centered Approach: Strong focus on supported self-management and accessibility (WCAG 2.1 Level AA compliance)
  • Phased Implementation: Well-structured 3-phase approach with realistic timelines

organisational Readiness

  • Leadership Buy-in: Digital transformation embedded at leadership level with dedicated team structure
  • Service Portfolio: Diverse range of services already "digital ready" for integration
  • Partnership Potential: Established relationships with NHS, health boards, and potential for TURAS integration

Weaknesses

Areas requiring attention and improvement to ensure successful implementation

System Integration Challenges

  • Fragmented Data: Multiple systems not yet integrated, limiting 360-degree view of activities
  • Service Standardization: High variability in service delivery formats makes consistent digital experience challenging
  • Legacy Infrastructure: Some digital assets require significant updates to reach industry standards

Resource Constraints

  • Team Capacity: Ambitious roadmap requiring significant input from existing team while maintaining BAU
  • Change Management: Risk of overwhelming organisation with digital transformation while delivering core services
  • Technical Debt: Multiple work packages needed to bring existing systems to required standards

Governance Complexity

  • Data Security: Complex volunteer/service user data sharing requirements, particularly with Assemble integration
  • Compliance Requirements: GDPR and clinical data governance adding complexity to digital service delivery

Opportunities

Strategic possibilities that could drive significant growth and impact

Digital Health Innovation

  • Service Excellence: Opportunity to deliver "world-class" supported self-management tools that set new standards in digital health
  • Scottish Government Alignment: Integration potential with Scot Account and national digital platforms
  • AI and Analytics: Data warehouse development enables advanced analytics and personalized service recommendations

Service Expansion and Integration

  • Healthcare Integration: Potential for seamless GP referrals through Vision integration and wider NHS connectivity
  • International Impact: Professional e-learning courses deployable globally, extending CHSS's reach and influence internationally
  • Cross-Condition Applications: Model expandable beyond chest, heart, and stroke to other chronic conditions

Partnership and Collaboration

  • TURAS Integration: Direct pathway into NHS Scotland's professional development infrastructure
  • Research Opportunities: Rich data collection capabilities for service effectiveness and health outcomes research
  • Volunteer Enhancement: Digital tools to improve volunteer experience and expand remote volunteering

Threats

External and internal risks that could impact successful delivery

External Pressures

  • Funding Constraints: Charity sector funding pressures potentially impacting investment in digital transformation
  • Regulatory Changes: Evolving data protection and clinical governance requirements
  • Digital Divide: Risk of excluding service users unable/unwilling to engage digitally

Technology and Market Risks

  • Rapid Technology Change: 3-year roadmap may become outdated by changing digital health landscape
  • Cybersecurity Threats: Increased attack surface with expanded digital footprint and service user data
  • External Developments: NHS and private sector developing similar digital health solutions that may impact service differentiation

Implementation Risks

  • Complexity Management: Risk of over-engineering solution leading to poor user experience
  • Change Resistance: Staff and service user resistance to digital-first approaches
  • Integration Failures: Technical challenges in connecting diverse systems and maintaining data integrity
